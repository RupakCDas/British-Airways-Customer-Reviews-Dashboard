# British-Airways-Customer-Reviews-Dashboard
## Project Overview
This Tableau dashboard provides a comprehensive analysis of British Airways Customer Reviews across an eight-year period (March 2016 – October 2023). Designed with user interactivity in mind, the dashboard allows stakeholders to deep-dive into passenger satisfaction metrics based on geographic location, timelines, aircraft types, traveler profiles, and seating classes.

## Key Dashboard Features & Visualizations
#### 1. Executive Summary KPIs (Top Banner)
What it shows: A high-level view of critical performance metrics across core service categories.
Current Insights: While the Avg. Rating stands at a strong 4.19, specific operational areas reveal significant room for improvement—particularly Avg. Entertainment (1.44) and Avg. Food & Beverages (2.38).
#### 2. Interactive Filtering Pane (Left Sidebar)
Dynamic Metric Selection: Features a parameter control (Select a Metric) that dynamically updates the entire dashboard view based on the chosen category (e.g., Overall, Cabin Staff Service, Value For Money).
Granular Slicers: Includes filters for Month of Date, Aircraft (group), Traveller Type (Business, Leisure, etc.), Seat Type (Economy, First Class, etc.), and Continent to allow for multi-dimensional data exploration.
#### 3. Geographic Heat Map (Overall Rating by Country)
What it shows: A global map tracking passenger locations. Darker blue shading represents regions with higher volumes or distinct rating patterns, helping identify key international market sentiments.
#### 4. Historical Trend Analysis (Avg. Overall Rating by Month)
What it shows: A continuous line chart tracking customer sentiment from 2016 through early 2024.
Current Insights: The chart highlights noticeable volatility over the years, with a distinct, sharp dip in customer satisfaction and review consistency occurring around the 2020–2021 timeframe (likely correlating with global travel disruptions).
#### 5. Dual-Axis Performance Matrix (Avg. Overall Rating by Aircraft)
What it shows: A side-by-side horizontal bar chart comparing the average customer rating against the volume (Number of Reviews) for individual aircraft groups.
Current Insights: This view isolates product performance. For instance, the A350 holds the highest average rating (5.9), though based on a small sample size (4 reviews). Meanwhile, workhorse models like the A320 and Boeing 777 show steady, reliable ratings (4.3 and 4.4, respectively) across significantly higher review volumes.
